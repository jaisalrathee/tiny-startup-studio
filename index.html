03 — Process

One week, four deliberate beats.

No discovery sprint. No 40-page brief. One discovery call on Monday, then async execution for the rest of the week. Loom updates instead of standups.

Day 01 · Monday

Brief & bet

A 30-minute discovery call to agree on the one thing that has to work. I write a one-pager. You approve it before lunch.

Day 02 · Tuesday

Frame

Schemas, key screens, the data model. A clickable shell of the product is in your inbox by EOD.

Day 03–04 · Wed–Thu

Build

Heads-down. Loom updates twice a day. You poke at preview deploys on your own time and leave async notes.

Day 05 · Friday

Ship

Production deploy, custom domain, a Loom walkthrough, and the keys handed over. Delivered within a week.

05 — Questions

Things people usually ask.

/ 01Can you really ship a real product in a week?
Yes — provided the scope is honest. I'm ruthless about cutting v1 to "the one thing this product is for." If your idea genuinely needs more, I'll tell you and quote a Fortnight.
/ 02What does "fully async" mean?
One 30-minute discovery call to align scope. After that — no standups, no Slack pile-ons, no calendar tetris. Loom updates, written notes, preview deploys you poke at on your own time. You get your week back.
/ 03What stack do you build on?
Default is Next.js + Postgres + Tailwind on Vercel or Railway, with whichever LLM provider makes sense. I can match an existing stack if your team is picking up the codebase later.
/ 04Do I own the code?
Completely. Every project ships to a GitHub repo you own, with a README, env variables documented, and a Loom walkthrough of the architecture. No lock-in, no escrow.
/ 05What's the catch with AI-built websites at $1k?
No catch — but no auth, no database, no backend. It's a marketing site or landing page, beautifully designed and conversion-tuned. If you need login, payments, or user data, that's Sprint or Fortnight territory.
/ 06Do I get a warranty or support after delivery?
No warranty, no retainer, no support package — that's the deal. Fixed fee, ship the code, hand over the keys. If something's broken on delivery I'll fix it; beyond that, hire your own team or come back for a new sprint.
